GolfTracker Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-04-23

GolfTracker ("the app") is a golf tracking application for iPhone and Apple Watch. This policy describes what data the app collects, where it goes, and how you can request deletion.

What we collect

  • Precise location — your GPS coordinates during a round, including the location of each recorded shot. Used to calculate distances to holes and to build your shot history.
  • Coarse location — derived city/course information.
  • Motion data — accelerometer, rotation rate, and gyroscope readings from your Apple Watch around the moment of each detected swing. Used for ongoing development of swing detection.
  • HealthKit permission — required to enable 800 Hz raw-accelerometer data on Apple Watch. This is the high-resolution motion stream used by swing detection. Granting HealthKit is what makes swing detection work.
  • Round content — your courses, holes, strokes, scores, targets, and club selections.
  • Anonymous device identifier — a UUID generated on first launch and stored locally. This is how we associate your data on the backend without ever asking for your name, email, or any login.

We do not collect your name, email address, phone number, contacts, photos, or any social identifiers. There is no user account system.

How we use it

Data is uploaded to our backend, keyed to the anonymous device UUID, and used to store your round history across devices and to continue developing swing detection.

We do not sell your data, share it with advertisers, or use it for tracking across other apps or websites.

How long we keep it

Personal data (rounds, courses, scores, and the device UUID that links them to you) is retained until you delete it. Anonymized motion and location signals are retained indefinitely as research data.

Deleting your data

Open Settings → Delete my data in the app. This will:

  • Delete every record tied to your device — rounds, courses, strokes, scores, targets.
  • Sever the link between your device and any motion signals collected from it. The raw signal data is re-keyed to a fresh random identifier and retained in de-identified form for continued development of swing detection. It is no longer associated with you or your device.

Deleting the app also removes locally stored data and prevents further uploads.

Permissions

Location, motion, and HealthKit permissions are requested via the standard iOS/watchOS prompts. You can revoke them at any time in Settings. Revoking a permission does not delete previously uploaded data — use Settings → Delete my data in the app for that.

Changes

If this policy changes, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top and, for material changes, note them prominently in the app.
