GolfTracker Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-04-23

GolfTracker ("the app") is a golf tracking application for iPhone and Apple Watch. This policy describes what data the app collects, how it is used, and how you can delete it.

Your account data (rounds, courses, scores) is linked to your Apple Sign In identifier. Raw motion signals used to develop swing detection are stored separately under an anonymous per-install device identifier with no link to your account.

What we collect

  • Apple Sign In identifier — when you sign in with Apple, we receive an opaque user ID that Apple generates specifically for GolfTracker. We do not receive your name, email, or Apple ID — only the identifier. It is used to associate your rounds with your account across devices and reinstalls.
  • Precise location — your GPS coordinates during a round, including the location of each recorded shot. Used to calculate distances to holes and to build your shot history.
  • Coarse location — derived city/course information.
  • Motion data — accelerometer, rotation rate, and gyroscope readings from your Apple Watch around the moment of each detected swing. Used for ongoing development of swing detection.
  • HealthKit permission — required to enable 800 Hz raw-accelerometer data on Apple Watch. This is the high-resolution motion stream used by swing detection. Granting HealthKit is what makes swing detection work.
  • Round content — your courses, holes, strokes, scores, targets, and club selections.
  • Anonymous device identifier — a UUID generated on first launch and stored locally. Used only to key motion research data; never linked to your account.

How we use it

Your account data — rounds, courses, strokes, scores, targets, and clubs — is uploaded to our backend keyed to your Apple Sign In identifier. This is what lets your history follow you across devices and reinstalls.

Research data — raw motion signals from your Apple Watch and the approximate location at which they were recorded — is uploaded separately, keyed to the anonymous device identifier with no link to your account. This data is used to continue developing swing detection.

We do not sell your data, share it with advertisers, or use it for tracking across other apps or websites.

How long we keep it

Account data is retained until you delete it. Anonymized research data is retained indefinitely.

Deleting your data

Open Settings → Delete my data in the app. This will remove every record tied to your Apple Sign In identifier — rounds, courses, strokes, scores, targets, clubs.

Anonymized research data (raw motion signals) is not deleted. It is stored under the per-install device identifier and has no link to your account.

Deleting the app removes locally stored data and prevents further uploads. Signing out removes your session from the device without deleting server data.

Permissions

Location, motion, and HealthKit permissions are requested via the standard iOS/watchOS prompts. You can revoke them at any time in Settings. Revoking a permission does not delete previously uploaded data — use Settings → Delete my data in the app for that.

Changes

If this policy changes, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top and, for material changes, note them prominently in the app.
